Basic Overview

Here is a simple overview of the stages involved at the farm

  • Planting and Cultivation: Coffee plants are grown from seeds in nurseries, and once they’re strong enough, they’re transplanted to the main field. They require careful nurturing, including proper soil, water, and shade management.
  • Harvesting: Ripe coffee cherries are handpicked or mechanically harvested. This step requires skilled labour to ensure only the ripe cherries are selected.
  • Processing: The cherries’ outer layers are removed to extract the coffee beans. There are two main processing methods: the “dry” method, where cherries are sun-dried before extraction, and the “wet” method, which involves fermentation and washing.
  • Drying: If the wet processing method is used, the beans need to be dried to the right moisture content before they can be stored.
  • Milling and Sorting: The dried beans go through milling machines to remove any remaining layers, and they are sorted to remove defective or damaged beans.
  • Grading: Beans are sorted based on their size, quality, and density. This grading helps determine their value in the market.
